History of Tokyo

Tokyo, formerly known as Edo, became the political center of Japan in 1603 when Tokugawa Ieyasu established his feudal government there. Since then, it has evolved from a small fishing village to one of the world's largest cities. Tokyo officially became Japan's capital in 1868 during the Meiji Restoration.

Over the centuries, Tokyo has witnessed significant historical events, including the devastation of the Great Kanto Earthquake in 1923 and the firebombing during World War II. Despite these challenges, the city has continuously rebuilt itself, maintaining a blend of traditional culture and modern innovation.

Top Locations to Visit in Tokyo

Tokyo Tower

Standing at 333 meters, Tokyo Tower is an iconic symbol of Japan’s post-war rebirth and a popular tourist attraction.

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple, Senso-ji, is located in Asakusa. This beautiful Buddhist temple draws millions of visitors each year.

Shibuya Crossing

Famous for its bustling atmosphere, Shibuya Crossing is known as the busiest pedestrian intersection in the world.

Imperial Palace

The residence of Japan's Emperor, the Imperial Palace is surrounded by beautiful gardens and historical landmarks.
